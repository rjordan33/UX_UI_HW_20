This website received a facelift while also enabling visitors the opportunity to schedule a tour through the website.

DATE

Aug 2022 - Sep 2022

TEAM

Rod Jordan, Zeke Wolfe, James Moore

ROLE

User Research / UX/UI Design



Challenge ——

Hollyhill Hummingbird Farm, a 501 Nonprofit corporation, is an educational farm located in Santa Rosa, California. Its focus is on biodynamics, sustainability, and the ethical treatment of life. The farm was founded in 2011 by an aerospace engineer. The website appears to have been around since about that same time.


Redesign website to feature donations and tours to bring revenue to the website. Increase the web presence of the non-profit organization by updating website to 2022 standards.

Original Website ——

Last updated in 2012, the original Hollyhill Hummingbird farm website is violently centered and mostly text. We reached out to the non-profit organization but unfortunately never heard back.

Initial User Testing ——

Initial website testing revealed a strong interest in visitng and experiencing the farm as an all-in package. Most users requested a farm experience that was laid back over traditional hard labor.

Problem Statement ——

Young professionals who like gardening are unsatisfied with their career choices and need to find a place where they can learn agricultural skills, a challenge when land is unavailable and the learning curve is steep.

User Flow ——

01 — From homepage, user can access Experience the Farm which then directs them to Pick Your Tour.

02 — Users can select from four different types of tours (beer, farm, school, or group).

03 — When confirming the type of tour, user inputs personal information, party size, date & time, and special occasion if applicable. 04 — Once information is entered, details are reviewed and confirmed with success screen.
